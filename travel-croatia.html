Visual Essay — Croatia

"The coast here doesn't perform for you. You have to earn it — early mornings, wrong turns, and a willingness to get sunburned on a stone that's been warm since the Romans."

Croatia surprised me with its textures. The photographs I came back with weren't the postcards I expected — they were the stone walls of Dubrovnik at 6am, the green of the Plitvice water in a light that made it look unreal, a fishing boat at dusk with paint peeling off in exactly the right places.

"The coast didn't need drama — it already had it. My job was just to not get in the way."

Croatia is one of those places that looks like it was designed for photography, which paradoxically makes it harder to photograph. The obvious shots are everywhere. So I looked for the edges — the worn doors, the old men playing cards in the shade, the boats nobody uses anymore.

The light changes fast here. You have maybe forty minutes before golden hour becomes something ordinary. But in those forty minutes, the limestone turns amber and the Adriatic goes from blue to violet to something you don't have a name for.
