About

A lens pointed
at everything

"I don't photograph categories — I photograph feelings."

I'm Jeremy. Sixteen years old, German by roots, Slovak by life. I arrived in Lubeník as a missionary and found something unexpected — time. Time to slow down, to look, to notice the way light falls on things that most people walk past.

My work moves across landscapes, weddings, portraits, cars, fashion, events — wherever I sense something worth stopping for. I'm not trying to master a genre. I'm trying to get better at paying attention.

Photography and film are how I understand the places I end up in. They're less a career right now, more a compulsion. Every frame is a small act of curiosity.
